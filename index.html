About the Institute

The Medanta Institute of Liver Transplantation and Regenerative Medicine is Asia’s first of its kind dedicated Institute offering liver transplantation and all other levels of treatment for liver and biliary diseases including cancer, both in adults and children. The Living Donor Liver Transplant program is the largest in India and second largest in the world with a team of highly qualified doctors with a rich experience of more than 2,500 Liver Transplants, having groomed the programme from its inception. The program has achieved the highest success rates (95%) and lowest infection rates (< 10%) in Liver Transplant. The Institute is also a high-volume referral centre for all types of liver tumours, bile duct cancer, cysts and blocks and all complex non-transplant liver and biliary surgery. The Institute is committed to a scientific, compassionate and patient-friendly approach.

The DaVinci Si 3D Robot
The Da Vinci robotic technology is the most advanced platform for minimally invasive surgery available today. It enables surgeons to perform delicate and complex surgeries through a few tiny incisions, instead of giving the patient a big cut. The system has an ergonomically designed console where the surgeon sits while operating. The hand movements of the surgeon are converted into corresponding micro-movements of instruments inside the patient’s body.
Moduler Liver Operating Theatres
Specially designed state-of-the- art modular operating theatres constructed with seamless, anti-infection technology fit in all equipment and fool-proof monitoring systems required by the liver surgeon to perform any surgery with precision. 256 slice CT Scan
This is an innovative non-invasiv e imaging technique that is specifically used to obtain a complete 3-Dimensional visual representation of the liver and other organs, in as less as 5 seconds. Avail advanced treatment and procedures

Liver Transplant
Liver transplantation is the repla cement of an infected Liver with a healthy liver. Generally, the procedure is necessary when the liver functions are damaged beyond the capacity to regenerate. Living donor transplantation is a procedure through which a part of a living donor liver is removed and then implanted in the receiver. The benefit is that the liver regenerates to its full size in both the receiver and the donor.
Liver Surgery
Liver surgery include operations of the liver for various disorders like resection, it is the most common operation of the liver in which a part of the liver is removed. It approximately takes 3-4hours for the surgery and 5-6 weeks for complete recovery.
Biliary Surgery
This is an operation on the com mon bile duct, which connects the liver to the gastrointestinal tract. The bile duct transports bile, which breaks up lipids in our food so they can be absorbed by the body. In the event that the stream of bile from the liver is obstructed by a gallstone or a tumour, for instance, you may require biliary surgery.
Intervantional Radiology Procedures
Interventional Radiology proced ure is used for the treatment of liver tumours. It refers to utilisation of radiological image guidance such as X-ray, ultrasound, CT scan or MRI to accurately target treatment. Interventional radiology treatment begins with passing a needle through the skin to the target. It is also called pinhole surgery.

Success stories at Medanta

Medanta Makes History: Cadaveric liver gives new lease of life to child.

Bhumika was suffering from a disease that meant her kidneys and liver needed to be replaced immediately. For the first time India, a cadaveric liver was split into two and one part used in Bhumika's combined kidney liver transplant at Medanta. A combined team of specialists and surgeons from the kidney and liver department worked non-stop to give the child a new lease of life.

A Pakistani child’s journey to Medanta for a new liver

Muhammad Akif, a 6 years old child from Lahore was diagnosed terminal liver failure and had a few months to live. Then he and family made the journey to Medanta in Gurgaon. Dr. AS Soin and Dr Neelam Mohan and their teams from the Liver department performed a successful liver transplant on the child. Today, Akif is back in Lahore, living life like other children his age

New lease of life for a 4 month old from his mother

Following is the story of newly born baby, Astik, who was suffering from Jaundice since he was a few weeks old. Further investigations diagnosed that the infant had not developed bile duct inside the liver, hence it was having a detrimental effect on his overall health. The liver team at Medanta recommend a liver transplant, where a small part of his mother’s liver replaced Astik’s liver and the duct of the donor (Mother) was connected directly to the baby’s intestine. Astik’s case has become India’s first infant to undergo a liver transplant surgery that lasted for 7 to 8 hours.

Sheriyar, Pakistan

Sheriyar was suffering from Biliary Atresia since the age of 1 and weighed 5 kgs. His liver went into a stage of Cirrhosis and his mother donated 20% of liver and gave birth to her child again.

Liver Transplant Saves Life Of Adolescent Neeha In Emergency Condition

Following is the story of Neeha, a young girl, who was diagnosed with acute liver failure and was air lifted from Dubai to Medanta - The Medicity, Gurgaon. Watch how Dr. Neelam Mohan and Dr. Soin’s team worked to give Neeha an all new and healthy life.

Mother gives life to her baby, the second time

Here’s the story of a baby girl, Aaliya, from Dubai who was given a new lease of life after undergoing liver transplant donated by her mother. At a tender age of one, Aaliya was diagnosed with severe liver ailment along with jaundice which contributed in deterioration of her health and interfered in her growth. After diagnosis and various tests Aaliya’s mother was considered to be the suitable donor for the transplant. Post transplant Aaliya was discharged in a couple of weeks and is now leading a healthy infanthood.
